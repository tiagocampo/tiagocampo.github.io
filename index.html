About Me

As of April 7th, 2022, I am a Data Scientist at Itaú-Unibanco.

In addition to my professional role, I am also a proud father, physicist, programmer, and hobbyist sys admin. I hold a Ph.D., M.Sc., and Bachelor's degree in Computational Physics from the Intituto de Física de São Carlos

During the early years of my research career, I focused on high-performance simulations of semiconductor systems. I completed a Sandwich Ph.D., spending a year at the University of Regensburg in Germany under the guidance of Prof. Jaroslav Fabian, a leading name in the international spintronics community. I also worked as a postdoctoral researcher at the Universidade de São Paulo - Instituto de Física de São Carlos and the University of Regensburg. I finished my research career at the State University of New York - Buffalo - SUNY, under the mentorship of Prof. Igor Zutic, another distinguished figure in the spintronics community. My interests lie in the areas of high-performance simulations, computational linear algebra, financial systems, and, more recently, machine learning. I am proficient in Fortran and Python, although I can write code in essentially any language given some time.
